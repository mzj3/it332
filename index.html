Hacking Technique #1


Phishing is a deceptive technique where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to trick individuals into divulging sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, or financial details.

Typically delivered through emails, messages, or fake websites, phishing relies on social engineering to exploit human psychology.

Common targets include individuals, businesses, and organizations. Phishing preys on human vulnerabilities, relying on users to click on malicious links or disclose information.

Prevention involves user education, awareness training, and implementing email filters to identify and block phishing attempts.

Remember, a company's least trained employee is their strongest defense against phishing!


Hacking Technique #2


Social engineering, also known as human hacking, refers to all techniques aimed at talking a target into revealing specific information or performing a specific action for illegitimate reasons.

This is done by either impersonating an important client via a phone call to lure the target into browsing a malicious website to infect the target's workstation, or using IT technologies as support to psychological manipulation techniques to achieve an objective outside the IT realm such as banking credentials via a phishing attack to then steal the target's money.

Typically, the main focus of social engineering are high-worth individuals, high-profile employees, and high-level leaders. Criminals target people with high levels of access. They want access to what these individuals have.
